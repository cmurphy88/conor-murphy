You've got a Facebook page. You're on Google Maps. Your regulars know where to find you. So do you actually need a website?

It's a fair question. And the honest answer is: probably yes — but not for the reasons you might think.

## What a website actually does

A website isn't just an online brochure. For a local business, it's the place people end up when they want to decide whether to trust you.

Think about the last time you searched for a local tradesperson, restaurant, or service. You probably Googled them, checked a few results, and then — before you picked up the phone — you had a look at their site. If they didn't have one, or it looked like it hadn't been touched since 2012, you may have moved on.

That's what's happening with your potential customers too.

## "But I get loads of business through word of mouth"

Great — that's the best kind. But here's what happens with word-of-mouth leads in 2025: someone tells a friend about you, the friend Googles your name, and the first thing they see is either your website or nothing at all.

A good website doesn't replace word of mouth. It supports it. It's the thing that turns a recommendation into an enquiry.

## Google Maps isn't enough on its own

A Google Business listing is essential, and you should absolutely have one. But it has limits. You can't explain what makes you different. You can't show your work properly. You can't take enquiries or bookings. You're just a pin on a map with a phone number.

A website gives you space to tell your story, show what you do, and make it easy for people to get in touch.

## What about social media?

Instagram, Facebook, TikTok — all useful for reaching people and staying visible. But you don't own any of it. Algorithms change. Accounts get restricted. Platforms fall out of fashion.

Your website is the one piece of your online presence that you fully control. It doesn't change its rules. It doesn't bury your posts. It's yours.

## The practical bit

A website doesn't need to be complicated or expensive to do its job. For most local businesses, four or five clear pages is enough:

- A homepage that explains what you do and who you do it for
- A page about your services
- Some photos of your work
- A contact form or booking option
- Maybe a page about who you are

That's it. Clean, fast, easy to find, easy to use on a phone.

## So, do you need one?

If you want to be found when people search for what you do, look professional when someone checks you out, and make it easy for new customers to contact you — then yes. A website is still the best tool for all three.
